Alvin's Letter to Karen,Alvin
{p}
Commander,

How are you? I am well. Very well.

I am writing this at dawn on the third day. Just after closing the tome. My hands are trembling, but not from fear. From joy.

{p}
Commander, we were wrong.

The dead have not vanished. They have not gone anywhere. They have always been there. We simply could not see them.

Now I can see them. They must be around you too -- you simply have not noticed.

{p}
On the first day, the letters crawled. Across the page, like insects. I felt nauseous.

On the second day, the letters sang. A beautiful melody. I wept. I am a swordsman. I never thought a swordsman could be brought to tears by letters.

On the third day -- the letters entered me. I can see them even with my eyes closed. Even now. Covering the inside of my eyelids.

{p}
Oh, please do not worry. This is not an illness.

Rather, it was before that I was ill. Not being able to see, not being able to hear, not being able to feel -- believing that was normal.

Lord Erenos forced nothing upon me. He did not even say "read." He simply placed the tome and left the room.

The tome called to me. It knew my name.

{p}
On the second night, I tried to pray to Jure. Out of habit.

I knelt, clasped my hands -- and then I realized. What was Jure's healing, really? Closing wounds. Warding off disease. Nothing more. Jure treats death as an "enemy." And offers nothing to the dead.

Jure fears death. A goddess, afraid.

The tome was not like that. It does not treat death as an enemy. It does not forget the dead. It simply gazes upon the boundary between life and death, as it is.

I unclasped my hands and stood. I have not prayed once since that night. There is no longer any need.

{p}
Commander. I want you to read it too.

If you read it, you will surely understand my words. If you do not, this letter will appear to be nothing more than the ramblings of a madman.

That is fine. I would have thought the same, once.

{p}
There is one curious thing.

I am very happy now. I never felt this peaceful while in the Order.

I no longer need to swing my sword. I no longer have enemies. Because I now understand -- everything is connected. The living and the dead. You and I.

Oh, Commander. I wish you could know this stillness soon.

{p}
P.S. Lately, when I look in the mirror, someone is standing behind me. When I turn around, no one is there. But I am not afraid.

I am sure I simply cannot see them yet. I believe I will be able to soon.

I look forward to it.

-- Alvin
